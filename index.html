HEAT RESILIENCY IN ARIZONA

The Opportunity Project

In fall 2022, University of Arizona participated in The Opportunity Project (TOP) Sprint. The purpose of TOP Sprint is to bring together people with diverse knowledge and backgrounds to rapidly prototype digital solutions ,powered by federal data, to solve a real-world challenge. This year TOP Sprint teams were tasked with creating a digital tool to help collaborators visualize the risks and effects of climate change within our community.

Heat resilience and house stability

Heat resilience is the ability to create and sustain an environment that can endure and resist extreme heat events. Since extreme heat events are increasing globally, and specifically in Arizona and the American southwest, we have compiled federal and state data to create an interactive tool to examine the effects of extreme heat on those who are most vulnerable, and to link to available resources in the community that can provide support during extreme heat events.
